What is a digital business card?

A digital business card is an online profile that replaces the traditional paper card. Instead of handing someone a piece of card they will probably lose, you share a link or let them scan a QR code — and your full contact details, photo, social profiles and booking links land straight in their phone.

Unlike paper, a digital business card is dynamic. Promoted? Changed your number? Edit once and everyone who ever received your card sees the new version. It also works both ways: with lead capture enabled, people you meet can leave their details with you, so a handshake becomes pipeline instead of a forgotten stack of cards.

CompanyCard cards work on any device with no app required for the receiver, include an email signature and video-call background for every user, and scale from one professional to a whole company with team management.

Five ways to share one card

QR code. The receiver points their camera at your code — on your phone screen, on a printed card, on a slide or a conference badge — and your card opens instantly. Nothing to install, nothing to type.

Personal link. Your card lives at a short, permanent URL. Drop it in a chat, a LinkedIn message, a proposal or a text — it works anywhere a link works, which is everywhere.

Wallet pass. Add your card to Apple Wallet or Google Wallet and share from your lock screen — faster than opening any app, and it works even with a dead network at a conference.

Email signature. Our free email signature generator turns your card into a clickable, branded signature for Gmail, Outlook and Apple Mail — so every email you send quietly shares your card.

Video-call background. A branded virtual background puts your name, role and QR code on screen in every Zoom, Meet or Teams call. Your card works the room even when the room is remote.

Who uses digital business cards?

Sales teams use them to cut the follow-up gap: the prospect saves your contact on the spot, and lead capture sends their details straight back to your pipeline instead of a pocketful of paper.

Real-estate agents, consultants and freelancers put booking links, portfolios and reviews one tap from a handshake — things a paper card physically cannot hold.

Recruiters and event networkers share with dozens of people a day without running out of cards, and can see afterwards which conversations actually looked them up.

Companies roll cards out to every employee from one dashboard with locked brand templates, so the whole team looks consistent on day one — see digital business cards for teams.

Frequently asked questions

Is a digital business card free?

Yes. CompanyCard's free plan includes a full digital business card with QR code, sharing link and unlimited updates. Paid plans add custom branding, lead capture, analytics and team management.

Does the other person need an app?

No. Your card opens in any web browser. The receiver scans your QR code or taps your link and can save your contact in one tap.

Can I update my card after sharing it?

Yes — that's the point. Your card is a live page: edit your details once and every QR code and link you've already shared shows the new version instantly.

How do people save my details?

Your card includes a 'Save contact' button that downloads a vCard (.vcf) straight into their phone's contacts — with your photo, title, phone, email and links.

Do digital business cards work on both iPhone and Android?

Yes. Because the card is a web page, it opens in any browser on any device. Both iPhone and Android cameras scan QR codes natively, and the vCard download works on both platforms.

How do I create a digital business card?

Open the CompanyCard builder, add your name, title, company, photo and links, pick a template and brand colours — and you instantly get a QR code, a personal link and a wallet pass to share. It takes about a minute and no design skills.

Are digital business cards professional?

Increasingly they're the professional choice: your details are always current, the receiver never types anything, and a branded card with a matching email signature and video-call background presents more consistently than paper ever did.

What happens if I change jobs or get promoted?

Edit your card once. Every QR code, link, wallet pass and email signature you've ever shared now shows the new details — no reprints, no outdated cards in circulation.
