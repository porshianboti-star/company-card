Virtual business card vs. paper: the honest comparison

The average paper business card is thrown away within a week — and the details on it start expiring the day it's printed. A virtual business card flips that: it costs nothing to "reprint", it can't be lost, and it gets smarter over time as you add links, offers and content.

Virtual cards also close the loop. When someone opens your card, you can see it. When they save your contact or leave their details through lead capture, that's a warm follow-up you would never get from paper.

Virtual vs. digital vs. electronic — what's the difference?

You'll see three names for the same idea. A digital business card is the umbrella term for any card that isn't paper. A virtual business card emphasises the remote-work angle — cards shared in video calls, chats and email rather than face to face. An electronic business card is the older term, often used for vCard (.vcf) files attached to emails.

Whatever you call it, the mechanics that matter are the same: one live page with your details, a QR code and link that never expire, one-tap saving for the receiver, and edits that propagate to every copy ever shared. That's exactly what the CompanyCard builder gives you — free, in about a minute.

In practice they're the same thing — an online card shared by link or QR code. "Virtual" emphasises that it works in remote settings like video calls and chat, while "digital" is the broader umbrella term.
