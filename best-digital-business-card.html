The four things that actually matter

Frictionless receiving

The best card is the one the other person can save fastest. No app for the receiver, one-tap vCard save, QR + link + wallet options.

Real brand control

Templates are table stakes. Look for locked company templates so every employee's card stays on brand automatically.

Team administration

Adding people one by one doesn't scale. You want central management, invite links and role-based access.

A fair 10-minute evaluation

  • Make a card on the free plan. If the free tier can't produce a card you'd actually share, the paid tier won't fix the culture behind it.
  • Share it with a colleague's phone. Count the taps until your contact is saved. More than three is friction you'll pay for at every event.
  • Check the team story. Ask: can an admin lock the brand, invite 50 people, and see every card in one dashboard?

Match the tool to your situation

Solo professional or freelancer: weight the free plan and the receiver experience above everything. You need one great card, a QR code, and a signature — not an enterprise dashboard. Start with a free digital business card and upgrade only if you feel real friction.

Sales team of 5–50: lead capture, CRM-bound contacts and brand-locked templates start paying for themselves quickly. Check the per-seat total honestly, including any physical accessories some vendors require per employee — that line item often doubles the real price.

Company-wide rollout: the deciding factors are administration and consistency: directory import (Google Workspace or CSV), role separation between admins and employees, and per-employee email signatures and video-call backgrounds so the brand shows up in every channel — see digital business cards for teams.

Event-heavy networkers: prioritise offline sharing (wallet pass on the lock screen) and analytics that tell you which events produced saved contacts, not just scans.

Frequently asked questions

What is the best digital business card app?

The one that saves your contact into the other person's phone fastest, keeps every employee on brand automatically, and prices fairly at your team size. Run a 10-minute hands-on test rather than trusting any vendor's comparison table — including ours.

Do I need an NFC card or is QR enough?

QR and link sharing cover virtually every situation and work on every phone. NFC hardware is a nice-to-have that costs money per employee and can be lost — CompanyCard deliberately works without it.

What should teams look for specifically?

Locked brand templates, central admin with invite links, per-employee email signatures and virtual backgrounds, analytics, and role separation between admins and employees.

Is CompanyCard free to try?

Yes — the free plan is a full working card with QR and link, no credit card required.

Are free digital business cards good enough?

For an individual, often yes — a good free plan covers a full card, QR code and unlimited updates. Teams usually outgrow free tiers because they need locked brand templates, central administration and analytics.

What do digital business card apps cost?

Most platforms run freemium: a free personal card, then per-seat monthly pricing for teams, and some also sell physical accessories per employee. Compare the total for your actual headcount including any hardware — that's where quotes diverge most.

How do I switch from another digital business card app?

Create your new card, then update the link in the places you control: email signature, LinkedIn, printed QR codes. Because CompanyCard links never expire, you only ever have to do this migration once.

What makes a digital business card good for the receiver?

No app to install, a page that loads fast on any phone, and a one-tap "Save contact" button that downloads a complete vCard. If the receiver has to work, the card has failed.
